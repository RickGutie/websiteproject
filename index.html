What is Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG)?

The Environment Act 2021 brought the requirement that all future developments must now produce at least a 10% uplift in biodiversity. The act aims to ensure that biodiversity is left in a measurably better state than before the development.

Development often results in impacts on and losses of nature. Therefore, mandatory BNG requires development to deliver more for nature, setting a requirement to increase biodiversity by a minimum of 10% compared to the baseline.

Biodiversity Net Gain (BNG) is already included in the National Planning Policy Framework (NPPF) and must be included in the planning application process. Moreover, 10% net gain will become a legal obligation for virtually all developments by 2023.
